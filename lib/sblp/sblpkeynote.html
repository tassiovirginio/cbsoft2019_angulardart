Keynote 1

Short Bio:


Beta Ziliani is a researcher and lecturer in the group LIIS at FAMAF, Universidad Nacional de Córdoba (UNC), and CONICET. He did a PhD with honors at the MPI-SWS under the supervision of Dr. Derek Dreyer, a and a MSc (licenciatura) at FCEyN, Universidad de Buenos Aires (UBA), under the supervision of Alejandro Ríos. Beta's research primarily focuses on improving interactive theorem provers (ITPs), and the application of ITPs to the verification of proofs. The Mtac projects (Mtac1 and Mtac2) are his most renown work within the community of functional programming in general, and Coq in particular.


Talk Title:

Using dependent types to build robust tactics in Coq.

Talk Abstract:


Coq is an interactive theorem prover (ITP) that contains a rich dependent type theory, using the Curry-Howard isomorphism to encode proofs as proof terms in the theory. Luckily, the user does not need to construct proof terms by hand: Coq also includes a tactic language, incorporating a more easy-to-use language to build ---and automate--- proofs. Like similar ITPs, the tactic language and the theory of the proof assistant are strictly separated, the former being mostly untyped, making tactics hard to build, maintain, and compose. The Mtac genealogy of projects (Mtac1 and Mtac2) brought a whole new concept to ITPs in general and Coq in particular: it mixes the two languages by encoding in a monad the primitives required to construct meta-programs (Mtac1) and tactics (Mtac2). Tactics written in Mtac's monad inherits the rich type system of Coq, to far-reaching consequences we are yet to explore. In this talk I will present our latest discoveries using dependent types to build concise and safe tactics.


Keynote 2

Short Bio:

José Valim is the creator of the Elixir programming language. He graduated in Engineering from the Polytechnic School of USP and holds a master's degree from Politecnico di Torino in Italy. He is co-founder and Director of R & D of Plataformatec, a consultancy based in São Paulo. He is the author of the Adopting Elixir, Phoenix Programming, and Crafting Rails Applications books. Valim also received the prizes of Ruby Hero (2010) and Erlang User of the Year (2015) and currently lives in Krakow, Poland, which has great summers, but the winters are very cold (at least for a Brazilian).


Talk Title:

Languages to Build Distributed and Fault Tolerant Applications with Elixir.

Talk Abstract:

In this talk, José Valim will give an introduction to the Elixir and the Erlang virtual machine and show how they use a new vocabulary for developers to build applications that are competing, distributed and fault tolerant. The presentation will also discuss Elixir's design goals and will include live demos.

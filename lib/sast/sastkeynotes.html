Gordon Fraser is a Full Professor in Computer Science at the University of Passau. He received his Ph.D. from Graz University of Technology, Austria, in 2007, then worked as a post-doc researcher at Saarland University, Germany, and as a (Senior) Lecturer at the University of Sheffield until 2017. He has published on improving software quality and programmer productivity at all major software engineering venues (e.g., TSE, TOSEM, ICSE, ISSTA, FSE, ASE, ICST) and has received six ACM SIGSOFT Distinguished Paper Awards (FSE'14, ISSTA'14, ASE'14, ASE'15, ESEC/FSE'15, ICSE'17), as well as best paper awards at SSBSE and GECCO. He has been programme chair of software engineering conferences (ASE, ICST, TAP, TAIC PART, SSBSE) and workshops, is a regular member of many programme and organising committees in the field (e.g., ICSE, FSE, ASE, ISSTA), is associate editor of the IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering (TSE) and Software Testing, Verification, and Reliability (STVR) journals.

Elizangela Paiva is a Senior Software Engineering Manager at Dell. Since 2008, she is an IT Test Manager for a global performance testing and quality engineering, managing distributed teams in Brazil, India and the United States. She joined Dell after 7 years working for Xerox, where she stayed 2 years living in Los Angeles in a global assignment project. She has a CSTE and Scrum Master Safe certification. Elizangela is passionate about coaching in leadership development career of young people and women. She loves traveling and meeting people from different cultures, and dreams of running the Sao Silvestre marathon.

Talk: Software Testing in a Digital transformation Journey
In this new area of digital transformations, testers are seeing a sea of changes on how they traditionally tests software in terms of skills, process and tools. Machine Learning/AI, devops, agile and continuous testing are disrupting the testing community, and forcing them to find ways to test faster and with excellence. In this lecture, Elizangela will share how to overcome the main challenges in automation and performance testing in this context, making the best use of technology such as bots and test tools.
